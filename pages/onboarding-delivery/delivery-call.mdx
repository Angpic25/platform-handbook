# Onboarding & Delivery Call

| Format | Duration | Tiers | Audience |
|--------|----------|-------|----------|
| Live call (remote) | ~60 minutes | Silver & Gold | PM, Dev, Legal |

This guide gives the PM and the Accessibility Expert a shared, structured framework for the 1-hour Onboarding & Delivery Call. It is intentionally high-level: it defines what to cover and in what order, not the exact words to use.

This call serves two simultaneous goals: **(1) onboard the client onto the platform** so they can operate independently, and **(2) present the expert audit findings** in a way that drives immediate remediation action.

<Callout>
The platform is not an automated scanner. The client needs to understand from the first minute that what they see is expert work — structured for action, not a generated report.
</Callout>

**Video demo of the pitch:** [PM Expert Platform pitch — Onboarding & Delivery Call](https://www.loom.com/share/9bec5c9c614d413b836bf2a5fccc11cb)

## Call at a glance

| # | Segment | Owner | Duration | Objective |
|---|---------|-------|----------|-----------|
| **0** | Pre-call prep | PM | Before the call | Platform ready, expert has access, top issues identified |
| **1** | PM: Platform Onboarding | Project Manager | ~20 minutes | Client understands the platform structure and how to read their results |
| **2** | Expert: Audit Findings | Accessibility Expert | ~30 minutes | Client understands the critical issues, impact, and where to start fixing |
| **3** | PM: Close / Next Steps / Q&A | Project Manager | ~10 min (within the hour) | Client understands issues lifecycle, and knows what to do next |

<Callout type="warning">
The call should not start until the audit is fully loaded into the platform. The client must never log into an empty system.
</Callout>

## Tier differences: Silver vs Gold

| | Silver | Gold |
|--|--------|------|
| **Expert support hours** | 1 hour (this call uses it) | 3 hours — more time for strategic guidance |
| **Retest** | Not included | 1 retest per touchpoint — explain when to use |
| **Audit depth** | Full WCAG 2.2 AA, 10 templates | Full WCAG 2.2 AA, 25 templates |

## 0. Pre-call preparation

These actions must be completed before the call starts. Nothing in this list takes more than 15 minutes.

| | Action | Why |
|--|--------|-----|
| ✓ | Verify that the platform credentials work correctly | PM and Expert will log directly live into the platform to present the audit — a broken credential is a bad first impression |
| ✓ | Review the top Critical issues in the platform — understand what the expert will present | The PM must be able to manage timing and redirect if a single issue consumes too much time |
| ✓ | Confirm with the expert which issues will be shown and in what order | The handoff between PM and expert must feel coordinated, not improvised |
| ✓ | Check whether the client's tier includes a badge (Silver & Gold: yes) and retest (Gold only) | These are common questions — be prepared before the client asks |
| ✓ | Set up screen sharing in advance — only the platform should be visible, no extra tabs | Professionalism of the demo matters — distractions undermine confidence |

## 1. PM section — ~20 minutes

<Callout>
The PM's role in the first 20 minutes is not to explain every feature. It is to give the client a mental model of the platform so that when the expert takes over, the client follows along — not just watches.
</Callout>

### 1.1 Welcome, introductions & agenda (~3 min)

Introduce all attendees (PM, Expert, client-side participants). Set the agenda explicitly — tell the client what will happen and in what order. Confirm who from the client side is attending and their roles. Review the [Procedure — Standards in client meetings](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1Iwbg4Dsw8xAXL1xiyL1onHtYZijvcnpY0eWKnvDdWZ8/edit?usp=sharing) for further details on how to properly set up and handle a meeting.

**Objective:** Everyone in the room understands the agenda. The client knows they will see the platform, then hear from the expert.

### 1.2 Platform introduction (~5 min)

Before opening the platform, explain what it is. Key messages to land:

- The platform is a **live dashboard** that presents the expert's audit results in a structured format — a live, interactive issue tracker connected to the client's workflow.
- The key advantage is having **humans in the loop**: the certified expertise of AccessiWay specialists is embedded in the platform. Every fix is verified by a qualified human.
- **Continuous accessibility lifecycle integration:** Jira & project management tools integration (next releases) — issues go directly into the dev team's existing workflow.
- **Multi-touchpoint tracking:** track progress across all touchpoints from a single view.
- Accessibility scores, highlights of major issues so that the client knows what to fix first, and suggested priorities to follow during remediation.

Platform walkthrough — high level and quick overview of the main sections:

| Feature | What it does |
|---------|-------------|
| **Home Dashboard** | Company level overview with key metrics and highest impact touchpoints |
| **Touchpoints List** | Browse and navigate all touchpoints in the account |
| **Touchpoint Overview** | Compliance metrics, trend chart, user impact, and most critical pages for a single touchpoint |
| **Issue Management** | Filter, sort, search, and export accessibility issues |
| **Issue Detail** | Full issue information with Overview, Fix, and Jira tabs |
| **Integrations** | Connect with Jira, Azure DevOps (coming soon) |
| **Settings** | Security (2FA) and team member management |
| **Platform Guide** | In-platform onboarding, feature explanations, and FAQ |

**Objective:** The client understands the platform's nature before seeing it. They will not mistake it for a generic SaaS tool.

### 1.3 Touchpoint overview walkthrough (~7 min)

Navigate directly to the Touchpoint Overview. Walk the client through four elements in sequence:

| Element | What to explain | Watch out for |
|---------|----------------|---------------|
| **Status Metrics** | Four counters: Open, Fixed, Resolved, Dismissed. Emphasise the difference between Fixed (client action) and Resolved (AccessiWay validation). Only Resolved issues affect the score. | This is the most common confusion point in the first weeks of use. |
| **Compliance Score** | It is the mathematical ratio between the number of criteria met and the total number of technical criteria that need to be compliant. It is a starting point that must be correlated with other metrics relevant to real accessibility impact. It only increases when AccessiWay validates a fix (Resolved), not when the client marks it as done (Fixed). | Clients often expect the score to rise immediately after their dev team fixes something. Clarify the Fixed vs Resolved distinction now or they will call you confused later. |
| **Trend Chart** | Shows how issue statuses evolve over time. At first audit, it shows one data point — no trend yet. This will become the client's internal proof-of-progress tool as they fix and retest. Exportable to CSV. | Set expectations: the chart becomes meaningful from the second reporting cycle onward. |
| **Issues by severity & Critical Pages** | Issues grouped by impact on end users (Critical blocks access, High impairs it, Low degrades experience). Critical Pages shows which pages concentrate the most critical issues — this is the starting point for prioritisation. | This section is the bridge between the platform overview and the expert's presentation. Use it to set the scene before handing over. |

<Callout>
If needed and asked, highlight that the compliance score increases by around 0.7% for each WCAG criterion completely solved (meaning no issues related to that criterion remain open).
</Callout>

### 1.4 Introducing the issues section (~3 min)

Navigate to the Issues list. Frame it as the team's **remediation backlog** — not a fault list. Point out the three most important columns:

- **Severity** — the priority signal. Start here.
- **Status** — tracks where each issue is in the workflow.
- **Type (Recurring vs Unique)** — a Recurring issue fixed once is fixed everywhere it appears. High leverage for developers.

Show how to apply Quick filters and Custom filters:

![Filter modal showing Quick filters and advanced filter options for severity, status, type, and WCAG criteria](/images/delivery-call-filter-modal.png)

**Gold tier:** briefly explain the retest here. The client has 1 included retest per touchpoint. Advise them not to use it too early — the best moment is after completing the Critical issues on the priority user flow identified in the Overview.

- Push the client to do the retest ideally within 6 months after the audit date. This allows Account Managers to handle the renewal phase more effectively.
- The status marked by the client as "Fixed" is visible immediately. The notification to perform the retest is triggered only when the client clicks the dedicated button.

### 1.5 Expert positioning (~2 min)

Close this section by handing over to the Expert. The transition must be explicit: tell the client why the expert is now taking over and what they should expect from the next part of the call.

Before the Expert speaks, the PM formally introduces them — briefly but meaningfully. The message to convey: the findings were produced by a certified human expert, not a tool, and the expert on this call knows the audit intimately.

## 2. Accessibility Expert section — ~40 minutes

<Callout>
The Expert's goal is not to list every error. It is to give the client a clear, prioritised reading of what matters, why it matters, and where to start. Quality of insight over quantity of issues.
</Callout>

### 2.1 Critical issues deep dive (~20–25 min)

For each Critical issue, open the side panel and walk through:

- What the problem is — described in plain language, not just WCAG code
- Who is affected — which type of user, what assistive technology, what happens to them
- Where it appears — is it Recurring (sitewide) or Specific (one page)?
- The Fix tab — show the expert-written remediation guidance

**Prioritisation logic:** Present Criticals first, then Recurring Highs. Within each severity level, tackle Recurring issues before Specific ones — the developer effort-to-impact ratio is better.

### 2.2 Selected High issues (~10–12 min)

After the Criticals, select 3–5 High issues to present. Prioritise issues that:

- Represent WCAG criteria not already covered by the Criticals — broadens the client's understanding of accessibility requirements
- Are Recurring — high developer leverage, fix once and the impact is sitewide
- Affect key user flows: checkout, login, contact forms, search — these matter most to end users and to regulators

Keep commentary focused on user impact, not technical depth. The developer audience will read the Fix tab — the PM and Legal audience need to understand business and compliance risk.

### 2.3 Recommended remediation approach (~5 min)

Close the expert section with a summary of the recommended remediation order:

| Priority | Issue type | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| **1** | Critical (all) | These block or near-block users from completing core actions |
| **2** | Recurring High | Fix once, resolve sitewide — maximum dev leverage |
| **3** | Specific High (key flows) | High-impact pages: checkout, login, forms, search |
| **4** | Quick wins | Issues related to the above selections with the same WCAG criteria — can be tackled quickly (e.g. html lang, skiplink) |
| **5** | Recurring Low | Wide reach, lower severity — batch these in a sprint |
| **6** | Specific Low | Lower urgency — schedule after higher priorities |

**Gold tier:** briefly remind the client how the retest works, summarising the PM's earlier explanation.

<Callout>
Important tip: use the WAA file on one screen and the platform on a second screen (the only one shared). Prior to the call, highlight in the WAA which issues will be presented during the delivery call.
</Callout>

## 3. PM closes — ~10 minutes (within the hour)

The PM retakes the floor after the expert finishes, or at any earlier point if the expert runs long or a question about process or contract arises. The goal is to ensure the client leaves with clear next actions and no confusion about how the platform works day-to-day.

### 3.1 Issue lifecycle (~3 min)

Explain the full loop in plain language:

Dev team fixes an issue → marks it as **Fixed** → AccessiWay retests → if correct, marks as **Resolved** → Score updates.

**Dismiss:** clarify this is not an escape mechanism. It is a conscious decision to archive an issue — it stays in the system. Appropriate for issues the client has evaluated and decided not to fix for a documented business reason.

### 3.2 Practical tools (~3 min)

Mention each tool briefly. Go deeper only if the client shows active interest.

| Tool | What it does | When to raise |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| **Export Issue List** | Downloads the full issue list as CSV (respects any active filters). Useful when the dev team works outside the platform or needs to share a list with an external agency. | If developers or external vendors are in the call |
| **Jira Integration** | Syncs issues bidirectionally with Jira Cloud. Issues appear in the dev workflow automatically. Status changes in Jira update in the platform. | If developers are on the call or the client uses Jira |
| **Retest (Gold only)** | Request expert re-validation after a significant round of fixes. Button: Ask for a retest in the Issues section. Only Fixed issues are reviewed. Best used after completing all Criticals on the priority user flow. | Gold clients only — give a concrete milestone, not a vague "when you're done" |

### 3.3 Identify the platform owner & send invite (~3 min)

Before the call ends, confirm who the platform owner will be. This is the person responsible for managing issues, updating statuses, and coordinating the development team.

- If the owner is on the call: invite them immediately from Settings > Team Members.
- If the owner is not on the call: get their name and email before the call ends and send the invite the same day.
- The owner then invites the rest of the team independently.

<Callout>
Each user invited to the platform receives an in-app onboarding video that covers all features. The PM does not need to do a full platform walkthrough on this call — the video covers it. Keep the focus on the audit results.
</Callout>

### 3.4 Wrap-up & explicit next steps (~1 min)

Close the call with clear, stated next steps:

- Confirm who is the platform owner and that the invite has been (or will be) sent
- The PM will send a follow-up email with a summary of the call, key points, and next steps
- The tutorial video will be available on first login inside the platform

**Gold:** confirm the next scheduled touchpoint and suggest a time for another technical Q&A — ideally 3 weeks from the first Onboarding & Delivery call. This pushes the use of the 2nd expert support hour and keeps momentum alive.

## Appendix: Messaging guardrails

These rules apply throughout the call. They protect credibility, manage legal risk, and keep the narrative accurate.

| Don't say | Say instead | Why |
|-----------|------------|-----|
| "We make you compliant." | "We give you the visibility and tools to manage compliance continuously." | Compliance is never finished. This creates false expectations and legal exposure. |
| "The platform fixes accessibility for you." | "The platform connects issues to the teams who can fix them and gives them what they need to act." | Resolution always requires action from the client's dev team. |
| "We offer an automatic solution." | "AI handles detection at scale. Humans handle judgment and complex remediation. That's why our platform is hybrid." | Overclaiming automation undermines the value of human expertise — our core differentiator. |
| "This replaces your need for accessibility consultants." | "The platform makes our experts' work more effective and gives your team the same structured approach." | Expert services are a core part of the value proposition, not a legacy item. |
| "The platform is fully launched." | "You're joining as an early adopter — with direct influence on the product roadmap and priority access to new features." | Transparency about platform maturity builds trust. Never commit to release dates or specific timelines. |
